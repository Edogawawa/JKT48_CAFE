SUSHI

Sushi
$12

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish that typically consists of cooked vinegared rice (sushi-meshi) combined with various ingredients such as seafood, vegetables, and sometimes tropical fruits. The most common seafood used in sushi are raw fish (such as salmon, tuna, and yellowtail) but also seafood like octopus, squid and eel are used. Sushi can be presented in many forms, such as nigiri-zushi (hand-formed sushi), maki-zushi (rolled sushi), and temaki (hand-rolled sushi). Sushi is often served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger. Sushi is a popular food worldwide, and many variations and fusion styles have been developed to cater to different cultures and preferences.

Ingredients:

- 2 cups sushi rice

- 2 cups water

- 2 tbsp rice vinegar

- 2 tbsp sugar

- 1 tsp salt

- Nori sheets

- Filling ingredients of your choice (raw fish, cooked shrimp, avocado, cucumber, etc.)

Instructions:

1. Rinse the sushi rice in a fine mesh strainer until the water runs clear.

2. In a medium saucepan, combine the water, rice, and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover and simmer for 18 minutes.

3. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar and salt. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the sugar and salt dissolve.

4. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl and add the rice vinegar mixture. Mix well to incorporate the vinegar mixture into the rice. Let the rice cool.

5. Place a sheet of nori on a sushi mat, shiny side down.

6. Spread a layer of the cooled sushi rice over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top.

7. Add your desired fillings on the center of the rice.

8. Use the mat to roll the sushi tightly.

9. Use a sharp knife to cut the sushi rolls into bite-size pieces.

10. Serve with soy sauce and wasabi on the side.
