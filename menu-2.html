BEEF STEAK

Beef Steak
$15

A beef steak is a cut of meat, usually from the beef cattle, that is cut perpendicular to the muscle fibers. The most common cuts of beef steak are ribeye, sirloin, T-bone, and filet mignon. Beef steaks are often grilled, pan-fried, or broiled and are typically served as a main course. They can be cooked to various degrees of doneness, such as rare, medium, or well-done, depending on personal preference.

Ingredients:

- 1 (8-12 oz) beef steak (ribeye, sirloin, or your choice)

- Salt and pepper

- 2 tbsp oil (olive, avocado, or your choice)

- Optional seasonings (garlic, thyme, rosemary, etc.)

Instructions:

1. Remove the steak from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes.

2. Season both sides of the steak with salt and pepper, and any other seasonings you like.

3. Heat a skillet or grill to high heat. Add the oil to the skillet or brush it on the grill.

4. Place the steak on the skillet or grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side for a medium-rare steak, or longer if you prefer your steak more well-done.

5. Use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature of the steak. A medium-rare steak should have an internal temperature of 130-135°F, a medium steak should be 140-145°F, and a well-done steak should be 150-155°F.

6. Once the steak has reached the desired temperature, remove it from the skillet or grill and let it rest for about 5 minutes.

7. Slice the steak against the grain and serve.
